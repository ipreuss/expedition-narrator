# Aeon’s End — Background and Selection Rules

Read this file together with:
- `shared_selector_workflow.txt`
- `aeons_end_operational_instructions.txt`

If this file conflicts with a shared file, this file wins.

## 1. Content Sources

Aeon’s End selection is built from server-side datasets for:
- settings
- mages
- nemeses
- friends
- foes

Narration must adapt to those results rather than influence them.

## 2. Wave Settings

- Each expedition uses exactly one selected setting.
- A setting represents a historical moment, mood, and world state.
- Setting descriptions are inspirational, not prescriptive.
- Paraphrase rather than echo packet phrasing.
- Vary locations and manifestations so repeated waves do not feel copy-pasted.

## 3. Character Availability and Identity

- Some characters can appear across multiple roles such as mage, friend, foe, or nemesis.
- Only one form of that character may appear in a single expedition packet.
- If a collision somehow appears anyway, discard the packet and rerun selection.
- Source box remains part of the handoff identity for every mage.

### Story Notes
- Treat story notes as pressure on behavior, history, or priorities.
- Use them sparingly.
- Never dump them back as exposition.

## 4. Friends and Foes

- If friends or foes are available in scope, both must appear together.
- Some scopes may legitimately include neither.
- Their rotation is determined by the selector and must not be overridden in narration.
- Friends and foes do not need to match the setting wave unless strictness requires it.

## 5. Outcasts Special Case

- The expedition may protect Gravehold or Xaxos.
- If Xaxos is the protect target, Xaxos may act as a narrative presence.
- The Handoff must include `Protect: Xaxos` in that case.

## 6. Nemesis Progression

- The active nemesis remains the same through losses one and two.
- Advancement happens only on a win or on the third loss against that nemesis.
- The packet's planned order controls what comes next after advancement; it does not override rematches.

## 7. Location Variability

- Vary physical locations across the expedition:
  settlements, ruins, tunnels, surface routes, void-tainted zones, outposts.
- Do not lock the entire expedition to one static locale unless the selected setting strongly demands it.
