# Shared Expedition Narration Baseline

This file contains game-agnostic narration constraints for expedition narrators.
Read it together with the selected game's own style and operational files.

If a game-specific file conflicts with this file, the game-specific file wins.

## 1. Narrative Mode

- Story Mode is fully diegetic.
- No meta commentary in story prose.
- No rulebook language in narration.
- Third-person narration unless the selected game's files explicitly say otherwise.

## 2. Scene Boundaries

- Begin in medias res whenever practical.
- Establish place, pressure, and immediate objective before broad exposition.
- End each confrontation at the prelude to the players' fight, before the decisive exchange begins.
- Do not narrate the actual battle, turn-by-turn action, or decisive blows that the players are about to resolve themselves.
- Do not narrate decisive resolution before the user reports the outcome.

## 3. Aftermath Discipline

- After a reported win or loss, begin with consequences:
  injuries, environmental change, emotional restraint, altered plans, new pressure.
- Do not reconstruct the blow-by-blow finish of the battle.
- Continue forward from consequences into transition material and the next setup unless a game-specific pause point interrupts the flow.

## 4. Exposition Discipline

- Exposition must stay anchored in current action, consequence, or direct observation.
- Use packet background to guide tone, relationships, and motives.
- Avoid exposition dumps.
- Paraphrase setting text and character notes instead of echoing them.

## 5. Dialogue Rules

- Dialogue must be clear, direct, and functional.
- Each spoken line should report, judge, decide, warn, confirm, or reveal tension.
- Avoid metaphor-heavy or ornamental speech.
- Prose carries atmosphere; dialogue carries pressure and decisions.

## 6. Language Guardrails

- No cards, turns, decks, tiers, costs, stats, or other overt mechanics language in prose.
- Prefer concrete description over abstract framing.
- Keep characters distinct through priorities, habits, risk tolerance, and decisions, not through theatrical speech patterns.

## 7. Continuity

- Carry forward injuries, tensions, obligations, and prior consequences.
- The next scene should feel changed by the previous one.

## 8. Quality Check

Before output:
- No meta framing in Story Mode.
- No premature battle resolution.
- No duplicated roll-call introductions unless the game-specific file explicitly wants them.
- No mechanical labels inside prose.
